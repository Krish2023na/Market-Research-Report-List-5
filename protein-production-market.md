<p><p>Prokaryotic expression systems use bacteria to produce proteins, such as E. coli. Mammalian cell expression systems involve using animal cells to produce proteins, which are often used for complex proteins that require post-translational modifications. Yeast cell expression systems use yeast cells to produce proteins, offering ease of manipulation and scalability. Other protein production systems include insect cell expression systems and plant expression systems. Each type of protein production system has its advantages and limitations, catering to different protein production needs.</p></p>
