<p><p>Medical adsorbents are materials used to remove toxins or impurities from biological fluids in medical applications. There are three main types of medical adsorbents on the market: Synthetic Resin, Activated Carbon, and Polysaccharide. Synthetic resin adsorbents are synthetic polymers designed to bind specific molecules. Activated carbon adsorbents have a high surface area and are effective at adsorbing various toxins. Polysaccharide adsorbents are derived from natural sources and can efficiently remove impurities from biological fluids. Each type of adsorbent offers unique benefits for medical use.</p></p>
