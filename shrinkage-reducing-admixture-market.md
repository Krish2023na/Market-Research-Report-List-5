<p><p>Shrinkage-reducing admixtures are commonly categorized into three main types: polyethers, polyalcohols, and others. </p><p>Polyethers are organic compounds that can effectively reduce the shrinkage of concrete by improving its rheological properties. Polyalcohols, on the other hand, are alcohol-based additives that work by reducing the surface tension of water in the concrete mix, leading to decreased shrinkage.</p><p>The "others" category may include a variety of different chemical compounds or proprietary formulations that are specifically designed to reduce shrinkage in concrete mixtures, each with their own unique mechanisms of action.</p></p>
