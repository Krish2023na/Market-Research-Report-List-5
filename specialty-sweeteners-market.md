<p><p>Specialty sweeteners are divided into two main types - natural and artificial. Natural sweeteners are derived from plants, fruits, and other natural sources, such as stevia, honey, and agave. These sweeteners are considered healthier alternatives to refined sugar due to their lower calorie content and lack of artificial additives. On the other hand, artificial sweeteners are synthesized in a lab and provide a sweet taste without the calories of sugar. Examples include aspartame, sucralose, and saccharin. Both types cater to consumers looking for alternatives to traditional sugar in their diet.</p></p>
