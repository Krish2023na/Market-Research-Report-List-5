High-flux dialyzers have larger pores to more effectively remove toxins, making them suitable for patients with high levels of waste in their blood. Low-flux dialyzers have smaller pores and are typically used for patients with fewer toxins to remove. The market for high-flux dialyzers is growing due to their efficiency in treating patients with severe kidney disease, while low-flux dialyzers remain relevant for patients with milder cases.</p></p>
