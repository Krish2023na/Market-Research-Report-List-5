<p><p>An osmotic pump is a drug delivery system that uses osmotic pressure to deliver a controlled amount of medication over a prolonged period of time. It consists of a core tablet containing the drug, surrounded by a semi-permeable membrane that allows water to enter the device and push the drug out through a small orifice at a constant rate. Osmotic pumps are commonly used to deliver drugs that have a narrow therapeutic window, as they can provide a consistent dose of medication over an extended period, reducing the risk of under or overdosing.</p><p>The osmotic pump market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.3% during the forecast period, driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, rising demand for targeted drug delivery systems, and advancements in drug delivery technologies.
