<p><p>Passive authentication encompasses two main market types: passive biometric authentication and device ID authentication. Passive biometric authentication utilizes unique physical or behavioral characteristics, such as facial recognition or fingerprint scans, to verify user identities without active participation. Device ID authentication, on the other hand, relies on information gathered from a user's device, such as IP address or location data, to authenticate identity. Both market types aim to provide seamless and secure methods for verifying user identities without the need for manual input or explicit action.</p></p>
